The making of GBA.js

Introduction

Hi!
I'm Maxime, french, 25 years old, I do front-end Web development for a living, and I am passionate about video games (especially those from Nintendo) and coding challenges. And here's my first public challenge:

Make a GameBoy AdvanceÂ© emulator in JavaScript.

Why the Gameboy Advance ?
Because I grew up with this handheld console and I like it a lot.
Also, online NES, SNES, N64, GB and GBC emulators already exist, so I'm taking the last free slot.

Why an emulator ?
Emulators are cool! Making them helps to understand how a console works!
Also, they are legal as long as they don't include any copyrighted material. I won't.

Why in JavaScript ? Because that's today's most interesting programming language!
With JavaScript + HTML5, modern browsers and handheld devices can read files, display 2D, 3D, make sound, use gamepads and save data locally. Even multiplayer gaming solutions are arriving. That's ideal!

And why a making-of?
Because I enjoy reading the story of this N64 emulator and this GB emulator, that are coded in JavaScript. They make me learn a lot of things, and I want to do the same thing: tell people what I do and help them learn from my project.

Is that all?
No: as a coding challenge, I'll write this emulator in pure JavaScript (no use of any external libs) and I'll try to make it as simple and lightweight as possible! It could also evolve and support Nintendo DS emulation someday.
